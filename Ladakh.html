National Parks in Ladakh


Hemis National Park


Location : The park lies within the Karakoram-West Tibetan Plateau alpine steppe ecoregion
Established in : 1981
Area : 4400 km2
Flora : Pine forests, alpine shrublands and meadows, and alpine tundra vegetation.
Fauna : About 200 snow leopards. Argali (Great Tibetan Sheep), Bharal (Blue Sheep), Shapu (Ladakhi Urial), and livestock. A small population of the Asiatic ibex is also present in Hemis. Hemis is the only refuge in India containing the Shapu. The Tibetan wolf, the Eurasian brown bear (endangered in India), and the red fox are also present in Hemis. Small mammals include the Himalayan marmot, mountain weasel and the Himalayan mouse hare.
Birds present here include brown accentor, robin accentor, Tickell's leaf warbler, streaked rosefinch, black-winged snowfinch, chukar, Blyth's swift, red-billed chough, Himalayan snowcock, and the fire-fronted serin. Among birds of prey noted here are Himalayan and Trans-Himalayan birds of prey: the golden eagle, lammergeier vulture, and Himalayan griffon vulture.
Features : It is the largest national park in South Asia.
