Kasu Brahmananda Reddy National Park


Location : Jubilee Hills and Banjara Hills in Hyderabad district
Established in : 1998
Area : 1.6 km2
Flora : Most of the park is covered with Southern tropical dry deciduous forest. The important trees here are Sandalwood, Teak, Babul, Neem, Ficus, Palas etc.
Fauna : Pangolin, small Indian civet, peacock, jungle cat and porcupines. There are over 600 species of plant life, 140 species of birds and 30 different varieties of butterflies and reptiles

Mahavir Harina Vanasthali National Park


Location : Vanasthalipuram , Saheb Nagar, Hyderabad, Telangana
Established in : 1975
Area : 14.59 km2
Flora : Dry deciduous forest mixed with scrub jungle and grasslands, sandalwood, rosewood, palas, amalthas, albizzias, accacias, teak
Fauna : Panther, black buck, cheetal deer, wild boar, civet, porcupine, peacock. There are 30 species of reptiles and over 120 species of birds.
Features : It is the largest green lung space in the city of Hyderabad.

Mrugavani National Park


Location : Hyderabad, Telangana. Near Chilkur Balaji Temple, about 20 km by road from Mahatma Gandhi Bus Station
Established in : 1994
Area : 4.90 km2
Flora : Teak, Bamboo, Sandal, Picus, Palas, Rela. The plant species include bryophytes, Pteridophytes, Herbs, Shrubs, Climbers and Trees. The vegetative cover presents a mosaic of woodland and grasslands. The plants of the park is of tropical waterless deciduous forest of degraded nature.
Fauna : Cheetal, Sambar, Wild boar, Jungle Cat, Civet Cat, Mongoose, Monitor Lizard, Python, rat snake, Russell Viper, King Cobra, porcupine, fox, black naped hare, panther, jackals.
Birds are quail, peacock, warblers, partridges, ducks, koel, curlews, flower peckers, etc.
