Gir Forest National Park


Location : Talala Gir, Gujarat. 43 km north-east of Somnath, 65 km south-east of Junagadh and 60 km south-west of Amreli.
Established in : 1965
Area : 258 km2
Flora : Dry deciduous scrub forest and dry savannah forests. It is the largest dry deciduous forest in western India. More than 400 plant species are present.
Fauna : The count of 2,375 distinct fauna species of Gir includes about 38 species of mammals, around 300 species of birds, 37 species of reptiles and more than 2,000 species of insects.
The carnivores group mainly comprises the Asiatic lion, Indian leopard, jungle cat, striped hyena, golden jackal, Bengal fox, Indian gray mongoose and ruddy mongoose, and honey badger. Asiatic wildcat and rusty-spotted cat are rarely seen.
The main herbivores of Gir are chital, nilgai, sambar, four-horned antelope, chinkara and wild boar.
The reptiles are represented by the mugger crocodile, Indian cobra, tortoise and monitor lizard.
The scavenger group of birds has 6 recorded species of vultures. Some of the typical species of Gir include crested serpent eagle, endangered Bonelli's eagle, changeable hawk-eagle, brown fish owl, Indian eagle-owl, rock bush-quail, Indian peafowl, brown-capped pygmy woodpecker, black-headed oriole, crested treeswift and Indian pitta.
Features : The most promising long term preserve for threatened Asiatic lion.

Marine National Park, Gulf of Kutch


Location : Southern shore of the Gulf of Kutch in the Devbhumi Dwarka district of Gujarat
Established in : 1982
Area : 162.89 km2
Fauna : 70 species of sponges are found. Coral 52 species including 44 species of hard coral 10 species of soft coral and almost 90 species of birds.Jellyfish, Portuguese man of war and sea anemones are other coelenterates found here. Arthropods include 27 species of prawns, 30 species of crabs, lobsters, shrimps and other crustaceans. Molluscs like pearl oysters and sea slugs are present. Octopus which change colour are also found. Echinoderms like starfish, sea cucumbers and sea urchins are present. The fishes found are puffer fishes, sea horse, sting ray, mudskippers and whale sharks which are an endangered species. Endangered sea turtles such as green sea turtles, olive ridleys and leatherbacks are seen here. There are three species of sea snakes. There are dugongs and smaller cetaceans like finless porpoises, common dolphins, bottlenose dolphins and Indo-Pacific humpback dolphins. Larger whales such as blue whales, sei whales are seen. birds species found here like crab plovers, sandpipers, western reef egret, great egret, ruff, eurasian oystercatcher, greenshanks, redshanks, gulls, skimmers, ducks, pelicans, storks, Godwits, terns.
Features : There are 42 islands on the Jamnagar coast in the Marine National Park, most of them surrounded by reefs. The best known island is Pirotan.

Blackbuck National Park, Velavadar


Location : Bhavnagar district of Gujarat
Established in : 1976
Area : 34.08 km2
Flora : Dense and sparse grasslands
Fauna : Blackbucks, wolves, Macqueen's bustards, hyenas and lesser floricans, with foxes, jackals, wild pigs, hares and rodents and jungle cats. Sandgrouse, harrier roost and larks are found among birds.
Features : Its semi-arid conditions, together with this inundation of seawater during monsoon, creates habitats suitable for various dependent fauna of the park.

Vansda National Park


Location : Vansda tehsil, Navsari District of Gujarat. About 65 km east of the town of Chikhali on the National Highway 8, and about 80 km northeast of the city of Valsad
Established in : 1979
Area : 24 km2
Flora : Deciduous forest. There are 443 species of flowering plants. This includes teak, sadad, khakhro, kadad, humb, timru, kalam, bamboo, dudhkod, mahudo, behda, umaro, kusum, tanach, asan, shimlo, ambla, sisam, chopadi bondaro, etc. There is a variety of colourful orchids at Ambika river.
Fauna : Indian leopard, dhole, rhesus macaque, common palm civet, Hanuman langur, small Indian civet, four-horned antelope, wild boar, Indian porcupine, barking deer, striped hyena, jungle cat, rusty-spotted cat, flying squirrel, pangolin and Indian giant squirrel. Pythons and venomous snakes such as the Russell's viper, cobras and kraits can also be found.
About 155 species of birds are found including common grey hornbill, grey-fronted green pigeon, yellow backed sunbird, Malabar trogon, jungle babbler, forest spotted owlet, shama, great Indian black woodpecker, are found. Apart from this, there is a variety of insects, centipedes, millipedes and snails. There are about 121 species of spider including the giant wood spider, the largest species of spider in Gujarat.
Features : Nestled in the Western Ghats of Sahyadri range, it has a unique flavour of flora and fauna population.
